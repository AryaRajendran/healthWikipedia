 In breast pathology, pseudoangiomatous stromal hyperplasia, commonly abbreviated PASH, is an overgrowth of myofibroblastic cells and has an appearance similar to fibroadenomatoid changes. The diagnostic significance is currently uncertain, but it appears to be benign. .]]The diagnosis of PASH is by biopsy. The important differential diagnosis is angiosarcoma, from which it was first differentiated in 1986. The management of PASH is surgical excision.
