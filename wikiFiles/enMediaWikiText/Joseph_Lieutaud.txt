 Joseph Lieutaud (21 June 1703 - 6 December 1780), was a French doctor. Joseph Lieutaud was born in Aix-en-Provence. He started studying botany, following in the wake of his uncle, Pierre Joseph Garidel, and went on to be called upon as a doctor in the Hotel-Dieu in Aix-en-Provence. He would constantly learn more from his patients and from the dissections he would perform on their corpses. By 1750 he became a doctor in the royal infirmary, then a pediatrician to the Louis XV court, and eventually the personal physician of King Louis XVI. He died at Versailles. He was also a member of the Royal Academy of Sciences and of the Royal Society of London, and President of the University of Paris. A street in the centre of Aix-en-Provence, is named after Joseph Lieutaud He published an essay on human anatomy.
