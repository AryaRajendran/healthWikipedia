The Journal of the American Osteopathic Association is a monthly peer-reviewed medical journal of the American Osteopathic Association. The journal primarily publishes original research publications and editorial articles. The editor-in-chief is Gilbert E. D'Alonzo, Jr. (Temple University School of Medicine). The journal is abstracted and indexed in PubMed/MEDLINE. It publishes case reports, clinical images, editorials, meta-analyses, original research, and review articles on all major areas of medicine, with an emphasis on the musculoskeletal system and osteopathic manipulative medicine. The journal also publishes content on medical education, ethics, and health care reform. The journal was established in September 1901. It was published bimonthly for the first year; starting in 1902 it was published monthly.
